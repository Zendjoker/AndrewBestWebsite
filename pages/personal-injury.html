Understanding Georgia Personal Injury Laws

Important legal information for injury victims in Georgia

Statute of Limitations

In Georgia, you generally have 2 years from the date of injury to file a personal injury lawsuit. Don't wait - contact an attorney immediately.

Comparative Negligence

Georgia follows a modified comparative negligence rule. You can recover damages if you're less than 50% at fault for the accident.

At-Fault System

Georgia is an at-fault state for car accidents, meaning the driver who caused the accident is responsible for damages.

Damage Caps

Georgia has no caps on economic damages, but limits non-economic damages in medical malpractice cases to $350,000.
