Georgia Family Law Essentials (Plain-English)

Equitable Division

Marital property is divided fairly (not always 50/50).

Custody decisions prioritize stability, safety, and involvement of each parent.

Modifications

FAQs (Quick Answers)

Common questions about Georgia family law matters

Q: Who gets primary custody?

A: Georgia applies the best-interests standard—stability, caregiving history, and each parent's ability to meet a child's needs matter most.

Q: How is child support set?

A: By guideline formulas using both parents' incomes, parenting time, healthcare, and childcare costs, with possible deviations.

Q: Can we keep it out of court?

A: Often yes. Mediation/settlement resolves most cases faster and cheaper—Andrew prepares like it will try, but aims to settle smart.

Q: When can I modify custody/support?

A: After a material change (job loss, relocation, health). Support can be revisited; custody changes require strong child-focused reasons.

Q: Are prenups enforceable in Georgia?

A: Yes, if entered voluntarily, with full disclosure, and not unconscionable at signing/enforcement.
